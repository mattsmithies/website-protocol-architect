---
title: "Why signatures are not enough"
category: "Cryptographic Architecture"
order: 1
---

A signature without context is fragile. A verification result without preserved proof is weaker still. Serious proof requires a preserved proof package — actor, key, algorithm, bound content, challenge, authority context, evidence, and anchor — in a form that survives time, movement, and changing assumptions without relying on the system that created it.
