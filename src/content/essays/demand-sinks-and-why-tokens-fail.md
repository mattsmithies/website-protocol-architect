A token without credible demand is a slow bleed. It does not matter how elegant the issuance schedule is, or how carefully the vesting is structured. If the system does not create genuine reasons for participants to acquire and hold the token, supply pressure will win eventually.

Understanding where value enters a token economy, where it leaks, and what structural conditions allow a token to hold relevance over time — this is mechanism design, not marketing. It requires mapping demand sinks against supply sinks, modelling issuance pressure, and honestly assessing whether the token plays a durable role inside the network or is simply a speculative wrapper around utility that could exist without it.

The systems that sustain long-term token value are the ones where usage, coordination, and incentives reinforce each other. Where supply pressure is understood in advance. Where the economic structure strengthens the integrity of the network instead of quietly undermining it.
