---
title: "Trust as explicit structure, not brand"
category: "Trust"
order: 6
---

In physical systems, trust is contextual and earned over time. In digital systems, it must be designed. You cannot rely on reputation or brand to guarantee that a counterparty did what they claim, that evidence was not tampered with, or that a process was followed correctly.

Explicit trust means verifiable evidence at every step. It means cryptographic proof where claims need to be independently checkable. It means accountability structures that are designed in, not bolted on after a breach. The organisations that understand this build systems where trust is a structural property. The ones that do not build systems where trust is a hope.
